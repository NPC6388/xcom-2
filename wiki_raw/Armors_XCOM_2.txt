'''Armor''' is a critical element of the game, providing resistance fighters with increased [[Hit Points (XCOM 2)|Hit Points]], additional utility slots, and eventually armor that reduces the magnitude of incoming damage. 

All XCOM soldiers begin with a set of basic [[Kevlar Armor]], which confers no benefits beyond allowing a single [[Utility Items (XCOM 2)|Utility Item]] to be equipped. Advanced armor sets ([[Predator Armor|Predator]], [[Warden Armor|Warden]]) require completing [[Plated Armor]] and [[Powered Armor]] research projects correspondingly and then purchasing the researched armor at the Engineering. These purchases are squad-based, meaning that these armor sets will be available to all XCOM soldiers after the purchase.

Specialized or unique armor sets will be available as [[Proving Ground]] projects after completing the aforementioned armor research projects or after conducting Alien bosses autopsies ([[Viper King Autopsy]], [[Berserker Queen Autopsy]], [[Archon King Autopsy]]). Each set of a specialized armor suit ([[Spider Suit (XCOM 2)|Spider Suit]], [[Wraith Suit]], [[E.X.O. Suit]], [[W.A.R. Suit]]) requires completing a Proving Ground project, 1 project per 1 armor suit set.

Resistance factions soldiers - [[Reapers]], [[Skirmishers]], and [[Templars]] - can wear only their [[Resistance Armor|faction-specific]] armor sets. These armor sets share characteristics with Kevlar, Predator, and Warden armor, but have fewer Utility Item slots ([[Reapers]] have 1 locked Utility Item slot by default, which can be unlocked only by acquiring a randomly available [[Tactical Rigging]] ability). [[Skirmishers]] armor sets have built-in [[Grapple (XCOM 2)|Grapple]] ability. &nbsp;[ [[XCOM 2: War of the Chosen|''XCOM 2: War of the Chosen'' DLC]] ]

[[SPARK (XCOM 2)|SPARK]] units are limited to SPARK armor sets which have slightly different characteristics than XCOM/Resistance armor sets, but totally lack Utility Item slots. &nbsp;[ [[Shen's Last Gift|''Shen's Last Gift'' DLC]] ]

===Health===
{{Main|Hit Points (XCOM 2)}}

All armor sets increase a soldier's '''Health''' value.

===Armor points===
{{Main|Armor Points (XCOM 2)}}

'''Armor points''' is a concept separate from ''Health'' and wearable armors, and represents a soldier's [[Armor Points (XCOM 2)|damage reduction]] capability. Only select models increase the ''Armor'' value.

==XCOM Armor==
Besides the general characteristics listed below, the division of XCOM Armor into sub-classes is mostly superficial. The only instance when it matters what sub-class an armor set/suit belongs to is having completed an [[Breakthroughs_and_Inspirations#Armor|armor breakthrough research]], which increases the HP bonus of a certain armor sub-class (Light or Heavy) by +1.

===Light Armor===
Light Armor grants the wearer extra mobility and dodge, focusing less on ''Health'' and more on the movement flexibility of the wearer. All Light Armor shares the ability to deploy a [[Grapple (XCOM 2)|Grappling Hook]] to reach elevated surfaces, and in extreme cases will allow the user to pass through solid objects with [[Wraith Suit|Wraith]].

===Medium Armor===
Medium armor is general-purpose armor which balances mobility with protection, allowing for an additional [[Utility Items (XCOM 2)|Utility Item]] to be equipped, while [[Icarus Armor]] is able to quickly shift positions and vault over objects and walls.

===Heavy Armor===
Heavy Armor focuses on the survivability of the wearer, increasing both ''Health'' and ''Armor'' points, and each possesses the ability to mount a single [[Heavy Weapon]].

==Resistance Armor==
[[Reapers]], [[Skirmishers]], and [[Templars]] each have their own [[Resistance Armor|faction-specific]] set of armor, which share characteristics with Kevlar, Predator, and Warden armor minus a few Utility Item slots (while [[Skirmishers]] have [[Grapple (XCOM 2)|Grapple]] ability by default). They are only available in the ''[[XCOM 2: War of the Chosen]]'' expansion.

==SPARK Armor==
SPARK Armor is exclusive to ''[[Shen's Last Gift|Shen's Last Gift DLC]]'', and can only be equipped on [[SPARK (XCOM 2)|SPARK]] units. It does not confer any particular attributes or abilities, but each iteration will grant additional ''Health'', mobility, and an extra point of '''Armor'''.
