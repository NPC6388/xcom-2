# Early × Research

**Phase:** Early (Gatecrasher → first Skulljack) | **Section:** Research | **Leads with:** Timing/tempo
**Difficulty:** Commander | **Scope:** base + integrated DLC, no WOTC

> Tempo thesis: research time is your scarcest early resource. Spend the **first scientist**
> on cheap enablers, get a **second scientist** fast, then commit the long block to **Magnetic
> Weapons**. Resistance Communications underwrites everything by unlocking contacts/income.

## Notes
- **Beat 1 (post-Gatecrasher):** rows 1–2 with your single scientist; staff the first new region with comms intent.
- **Beat 2 (after 1–2 contacts / 2nd scientist):** row 3, then commit to row 4.
- **Beat 3 (pre-Skulljack):** rows 5–6 once alloys/corpses allow.

### ⚠️ Conflict flagged (Magnetic Weapons timing)
- **Source A** (GamersDecide path): biotech → comms → **Magnetic Weapons** as the early firepower commit.
- **Source B** (a Steam commenter): *don't* rush Mag early — it doesn't save time; do short immediate-benefit research and **get a 2nd scientist first**.
- **Reconciliation for Commander tempo:** both agree comms + 2nd scientist come first. Recommendation: rows 1–3 → secure a 2nd scientist → **then** Magnetic Weapons (row 4). Don't make Mag your turn-1 long project.

### Prereqs — now locked ✓
Confirmed against the parsed darosh tech tree (see [tech-tree.md](tech-tree.md)):
Modular Weapons → **Magnetic Weapons** → Gauss Weapons; Hybrid Materials → **Plated Armor**;
Biotech (+corpse) → **Officer Autopsy** → **Proving Grounds** → **Skulljack** → Skulljack Officer → Kill a Codex.
The Skulljack chain (Officer → Codex → Avatar) is the real path to the endgame.
